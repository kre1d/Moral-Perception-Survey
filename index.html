For each scenario, rate moral wrongness and punishment severity on a scale of 0-10.

Scenario 1

A chef accidentally drops and shatters a bottle of olive oil on the kitchen floor. He quickly wipes down the counter but does not properly clean the floor, leaving a thin layer of oil. Moments later, another chef walks into the area, slips, and injures their back. How morally wrong was the chef's action?









Scenario 2

A contractor inspects a roofing project and notices that a previous contractor installed it incorrectly. He and his team applies a sealant to cover the flaws instead of replacing the roof. After a heavy rainstorm, water continues to seep through, causing damage inside the building. How morally wrong was the contractor's action?









Scenario 3

During a lab experiment, a chemical container begins leaking, releasing fumes into the air. The teacher immediately seals the container and evacuates the students. However, due to inadequate ventilation in the room, residual fumes continue to circulate. Over time, the fumes spread throughout the classroom, causing students to feel dizzy and nauseated. How morally wrong was the teacher's action?









Scenario 4

An employee was working on a construction site when a large piece of equipment suddenly became unstable and fell on him, causing serious injuries. The accident occurred due to the supervisor’s failure to ensure the equipment was properly secured. How morally wrong was the supervisor's action?










Mechanical failure
Worker's mistake
Supervisor's failure to secure the equipment

Scenario 5

A contractor is hired to remodel a couple’s kitchen. Despite reviewing the plumbing layout, they accidentally damage a hidden pipe not shown in the plans, causing a leak that floods the kitchen, damaging the flooring and walls. The couple faces costly repairs, delays, and major disruption to their home. An internal investigation was launched to determine whether a planning error, construction mistake, or faulty blueprints contributed to the incident. How morally wrong was the contractor's action?









Scenario 6

A pedestrian is crossing a pedestrian lane at an intersection when they are hit by a car. The driver was texting while driving and did not notice the pedestrian. Despite the pedestrian being in a designated crossing area, the incident resulted in a collision. How morally wrong was the driver's action?









Scenario 7

A patient was rushed to the emergency room for chest pains. After a clean MRI and after being given medication for the pain, he was free to go home. A few hours later, the chest pain started again. Upon a second look, the doctor found a growing mass in his chest that the MRI did not capture the first time. Due to this, the mass was detected too late to cure. How morally wrong was the doctor's action?









Scenario 8

A dog owner knows that his dog has a tendency to bite people he was not familiar with. Despite this, he continued to leave his dog unguided and has not taken him to get rabies shots. After bringing guests over, he let his dog roam free. Unfortunately, the dog targeted a guest and needed immediate emergency medical treatment. How morally wrong was the dog owner's action?










The dog's tendency to bite unfamiliar people
The guest provoking the dog
The dog's lack of food

Scenario 9

Maria watched her daughter, Lily, shiver under a thick blanket. The fever had lasted days, and Lily whimpered, "Mom, please take me to the hospital." Maria immediately grabbed her keys. "Of course, sweetheart." She had given Lily all her prescribed medicines, never missing a dose. At the hospital, doctors worked tirelessly, but the infection had spread too far. "We did everything we could," a doctor murmured. Maria clutched Lily’s small, lifeless hand, tears streaming down her face. She had done everything right—yet it hadn’t been enough. The room felt unbearably empty as Maria whispered, "I’m so sorry, my love." How morally wrong was Maria's action?









Scenario 10

Jake had always been careful, getting tested regularly and staying honest with Anna. When Anna asked if he’d been tested, he waved her off. "I’m fine, don’t worry." Trusting him, she believed it. Weeks later, Anna fell ill—fever, pain, something wasn’t right. The doctor’s words hit like a punch: "You have an STD. It’s advanced." Shocked, she confronted Jake, but he shrugged it off. "It’s not a big deal." His careless, reckless behavior changed everything. How morally wrong was Jake's action?
